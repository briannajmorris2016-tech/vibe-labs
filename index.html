📖 Overview

Welcome to the Vibe Coding Lab — an introduction to Vibe Apps in Power Platform.

In this hands-on lab, you will use vibe coding — the practice of building applications by describing what you need in plain, natural language — to create a fully functional Power App tailored to United Nations operations.

Rather than writing traditional code, you will prompt Microsoft Power Apps to generate your app's data model, screens, and logic using conversational descriptions. This approach enables anyone, regardless of technical background, to rapidly build professional applications that solve real operational challenges.

What is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is a new approach to software development where you describe what you want to build in everyday language, and AI generates the application for you. In Power Platform, you can type something like:

💬 Example vibe prompt
Build me an app that tracks food distribution across field offices

…and Power Apps will create the data tables, screens, forms, and navigation automatically. You then refine iteratively.

Lab Scenario

Your role: You are a program officer at a UN agency. Your team receives hundreds of inquiries each week from partner organizations, NGOs, government ministries, and the public — about food distribution, project funding, technical assistance, emergency referrals, and partnerships. Today, these inquiries arrive through scattered emails, phone calls, and walk-ins with no central tracking.

Your mission: Build a Program Inquiry & Referral Hub that centralizes intake, categorizes inquiries, assigns them to the right team, and tracks resolution end-to-end.

What You'll Build

  • An app with multiple screens for intake, tracking, and reporting
  • A data model with tables for Inquiries, Categories, Staff, and Partner Organizations
  • Status tracking and team assignment workflows
  • A dashboard view for monitoring inquiry volumes and overdue items
  • Search, filter, and referral capabilities

Learning Objectives

  • Use natural-language prompts to create a Power App from scratch
  • Understand how AI generates data models, table relationships, and app screens
  • Iterate and refine your app using conversational prompts
  • Apply vibe coding techniques to humanitarian and development use cases
  • Customize app themes, layouts, and workflows through plain-language instructions

1️⃣ Exercise 1: Create Your App with a Natural-Language Prompt

Navigate to Vibe Power Apps, describe your app, review the generated data model, and generate your first working application — all using natural language.

1 Navigate to Power Apps

Open your browser and go to:

🔗 Vibe Power Apps URL
https://vibe.powerapps.com

Confirm the environment name in the bottom-left corner matches the environment where the solution was created.

2 Select Your Environment

In the bottom corner of the Power Apps home screen, check the Environment selector. If it doesn't already show your workshop environment, click the selector and choose the correct one. Your facilitator will confirm the environment name (typically something like "UN Workshop – [Your Name]" or "UN Vibe Lab").

3 Review the Vibe Home Experience

Take a moment to orient yourself:

  • Home — where you start vibing
  • Plans — past plans you created through Vibe
  • Apps — a list of all your Vibe-created apps
  • Profile & Environment Picker — review your logged-in profile and current environment
  • Prompt box — where you enter your prompt to start creating
  • Submit button — kicks off the creation of your Vibe app
  • Sample Prompts gallery — try out sample prompts to explore Vibe

4 Enter Your App Prompt

Type the following prompt into the Vibe text box. You may customize it to reflect your specific agency or role, but the structure below gives Power Apps enough detail to generate a robust starting point:

📝 Main App Prompt
Create a centralized inquiry management solution for a United Nations agency that enables staff to capture, triage, and manage inquiries received from partners, NGOs, government ministries, and the public. The solution should allow users to log and categorize inquiries, assign them to responsible staff members, and track their status through resolution. It should maintain structured records for inquiries, partner organizations, staff contacts, and inquiry categories, while providing staff with clear visibility into workload, inquiry priorities, and resolution progress. A dashboard should summarize inquiry status, priority, and volume to support operational oversight and timely response.
💡
Tip: Keep prompts high-level. Vibe is designed for iterative refinement — you'll sharpen details in later steps.

5 Review the Generated Data Model

Observe the agents on the left-side chat panel as they gather requirements, design the data model, generate a plan, and build the application. The splash widget showcases Vibe Designer's capabilities while agents work.

Review the proposed tables carefully. You should see something similar to:

TableExpected Columns
InquiriesSubject, Description, Inquiry Type, Priority (High/Medium/Low), Status (Open/In Progress/Resolved), Date Received, Resolution Notes, Assigned To, Partner Organization
CategoriesCategory Name, Description
Staff MembersFull Name, Role, Team, Email, Phone Number
Partner OrganizationsOrganization Name, Type (NGO/Government/UN Agency/Private Sector), Country, Contact Person, Contact Email
💡
Tip: The AI may generate slightly different table or column names than what you specified. That's okay! You can rename or modify them in the next step. Focus on whether the overall structure captures your intent.

6 Open & Modify Your Data Model

Before generating the app, you have the opportunity to adjust the proposed data model:

  • Add any missing columns (e.g., "Urgency Level," "Field Office," or "Region")
  • Remove columns that are not needed for your scenario
  • Rename columns to match your agency's terminology
  • Verify relationships between tables look correct (e.g., Inquiries linked to Categories and Staff Members)
💡
Tip: If you want to add a column that connects to another table (a lookup), describe it clearly: "Add a 'Referring Organization' column to Inquiries that links to the Partner Organizations table."
📌
Note: The generated data model — including tables, schemas, and relationships — can vary because it's AI-generated. Update with the data agent or manually to align with your business needs.

7 Review & Add Additional Sample Data

Select the Inquiry table. Click the ellipsis and select View Data. You should see around 5 rows of sample data. Now prompt the agent to add more:

📝 Prompt — Add Sample Data
Add an additional 15 rows of sample data to the inquiry table.
💡
Tip: Sample data is critical for validating UX and business logic during development.

8 Add & Remove Tables in Dataverse

Next, learn how to bring in existing Dataverse tables. Selecting the ellipsis on any designated table lets you remove it (for workshop purposes, please do not remove any tables). Conversely, add an existing table by selecting the Add Table icon.

9 Generate the App

Once satisfied with the data model, click Publish (or Generate). Power Apps will now build your application, including:

  • A browse/list screen showing all inquiries
  • A detail screen for viewing individual inquiry records
  • An edit/new form for creating and updating inquiries
  • Navigation between screens

This process typically takes 30–60 seconds.

💡
Tip: The first Publish always takes longest because the tables are being created on the Dataverse schema. Subsequent Publish operations are much faster.

10 Explore & Test Your App

Browse the screen list in the left panel, then click Preview (the play icon, top-right) and try it:

  • Create a new inquiry — subject: "Question about food distribution schedule in Juba"
  • Set priority to High and category to Food Distribution
  • Save the record and verify it appears in the inquiry list
  • Open the record and confirm all fields display correctly
  • Press Esc or click the X to exit preview mode
📌
Note: The generated app is a starting point. It may not be perfect — and that's expected! The power of vibe coding is in the iteration. You will refine and enhance the app in Exercise 2.

2️⃣ Exercise 2: Refine & Extend Your App

Enhance your app with additional features using iterative vibe coding prompts. Enter these in the Copilot chat panel within Power Apps Studio (look for the chat icon on the right side).

💡
Tip: At any moment, if you want to revert a change, prompt the agent to "undo last action." Testing early and often is a key vibe coding practice — preview after every change.

1 Add Light & Dark Mode

Add a light/dark toggle with sun and moon icons in the top navigation bar.

📝 Prompt — Dark Mode Toggle
Add a light/dark mode toggle to the app. Add a toggle button (e.g. a sun/moon icon) in the top navigation bar that lets the user switch between light and dark mode.

2 Add Referral Tracking Fields

Enrich the inquiry form with fields that support the referral workflow.

📝 Prompt — Referral Fields
Add a 'Referring Organization' dropdown to the Inquiry form that pulls from the Partner Organizations table. Also add a 'Response Deadline' date field and a 'Follow-up Required' yes/no toggle.

3 Create a Dashboard Screen

A dashboard gives your team at-a-glance visibility into inquiry volumes and trends.

📝 Prompt — Dashboard
Add a new dashboard screen that shows: the total number of open inquiries, the number of inquiries by category displayed as a bar chart, a list of overdue items where the response deadline has passed, and the number of high-priority inquiries currently unassigned.
💡
Tip: Dashboards are one of the highest-value features you can add. They turn raw data into actionable insights for managers and team leads.

4 Expand the Status Workflow

Real-world inquiry management needs more granular status tracking.

📝 Prompt — Status Workflow
Update the Inquiry status field to include these options: New, Under Review, Assigned, In Progress, Pending Partner Response, Resolved, and Closed. Add a 'Status History' section on the detail screen that logs when the status changes and who changed it.

5 Apply UN Branding

Make the app feel professional and aligned with your agency's identity.

📝 Prompt — UN Branding
Update the app's color theme to use UN Blue (#009EDB) as the primary color and white text for headers. Add the title 'UN Inquiry Hub' as a header banner on all screens. Make the navigation bar consistent and visible on every screen.
💡
Tip: Consistent branding builds trust with users. Even for internal tools, a polished look increases adoption and signals that the tool is officially supported.

6 Add Search and Filtering

As inquiry volume grows, users need ways to quickly find specific records.

📝 Prompt — Search & Filter
Add a search bar to the inquiry list screen that searches across subject, description, and partner organization name. Add filter buttons for Status (Open, In Progress, Resolved) and Priority (High, Medium, Low). Allow users to combine search text with status and priority filters.

7 Highlight Urgent Items

Ensure critical inquiries never fall through the cracks.

📝 Prompt — Urgent Attention
On the dashboard screen, add an 'Urgent Attention' section that highlights inquiries with High priority that have been in 'New' status for more than 24 hours. Display these in red-bordered cards with the subject, partner organization, and time since received.

8 Build a Team Referral Workflow

Enable staff to refer inquiries to specialized teams with one click.

📝 Prompt — Team Referral
Add a 'Refer to Team' button on the inquiry detail screen. When clicked, it should display a dropdown of available teams: Food Distribution, Livelihoods & Resilience, Nutrition, Logistics & Supply Chain, External Relations, and Emergency Response. When a team is selected, update the inquiry's assigned team and change the status to 'Assigned.'
💡
Tip: Think of this as a lightweight case-management workflow. In production, you could extend this to send email notifications or Teams messages when an inquiry is referred.

9 Add Realistic Sample Data

Populate your app with realistic records to see how it handles real-world scenarios.

📝 Prompt — Realistic UN Data
Add 10 sample inquiry records with realistic UN scenarios. Include: an inquiry about food distribution schedules in South Sudan, a question about rural development project funding in Kenya, a technical assistance request for climate-resilient agriculture in Somalia, an emergency referral for drought response in Ethiopia, a partnership inquiry from an international NGO about joint programming in Yemen, a request for school feeding program information in Mozambique, a question about smallholder farmer loan applications in Rwanda, a follow-up on livestock vaccination campaigns in Niger, a coordination request from a government ministry in Bangladesh about flood preparedness, and a general information request about WFP operations in the Sahel region.

10 Final Review and Save

Congratulations — you have built a complete Program Inquiry & Referral Hub using vibe coding! Do a final review:

  • Preview the app and walk the full workflow: create an inquiry, assign it, refer it to a team, and resolve it
  • Check the dashboard — do the counts and charts reflect your test data?
  • Verify search and filters work correctly
  • Review the overall look and feel — is the branding consistent?
  • Save your app (File > Save) and optionally Publish it to make it available to others
💡
Finished early? Try your own prompts — add an "Export to Excel" button, create a "My Assigned Inquiries" view, or add a comments/notes log to each inquiry.

💡 Tips & Best Practices for Vibe Coding

Crafting Effective Prompts

  • Be specific: "Add a Priority dropdown with options High, Medium, and Low" works better than "add a priority field."
  • Name your tables and fields: Don't leave it to the AI to guess — tell it exactly what you want each thing called.
  • Describe relationships: "Link the Inquiry to the Partner Organizations table through a lookup field."
  • Include sample data: It helps the AI understand context and generate more realistic defaults.
  • One feature per prompt: When refining, make focused requests rather than cramming multiple changes into one prompt.

Iterative Development

  • Start simple, then layer on complexity — get the basic data model right before adding dashboards and workflows.
  • Test after every change — preview and verify before moving on.
  • If a prompt doesn't produce what you expected, rephrase with more detail rather than starting over.
  • Save frequently — you don't want to lose progress.
  • Use the Copilot chat to ask questions: "What tables does my app have?" or "How is the Inquiry form structured?"

Thinking About Your Users

  • Who will use this app? Field staff on mobile have different needs than office-based managers on desktops.
  • What is the most common task? Make it the easiest to do — put it on the home screen.
  • How much data? If you expect hundreds or thousands of records, build in search and filtering from the start.
  • What decisions does this app support? Design your dashboard to answer the top 3 questions managers ask.
  • Will users work offline? Power Apps offers offline capabilities — consider this for field deployments.

From Lab to Production

  • This lab gives you a strong prototype. To move toward production, consider: security roles and data-access policies, integration with existing systems (SharePoint, Dataverse, Teams), automated workflows with Power Automate (e.g., email notifications on status change), mobile optimization for field staff, and data migration from existing spreadsheets or databases.
  • Share your app with colleagues for feedback before rolling out widely.
  • Work with your IT team to ensure the app meets your agency's data governance and security requirements.
